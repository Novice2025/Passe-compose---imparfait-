🧠 Core Philosophy: Story vs. Scenery

French does not classify actions simply by "when they happened on a timeline". Instead, it asks a single cognitive question: "Is this the story event, or is this the background scenery?"

The Cinematic Dual Model

📸 Passé Composé
Snapshot / Flash
Completed Event
🎥 Imparfait
Video / Continuous
Background / Habit / State

"Il pleuvait (🎥) quand je suis sorti (📸)." — It was raining when I went out.

Passé Composé: Completed Events & Story Blocks

Used for actions that are finished, move the main plot forward, or act as sudden interruptions.

J’ai mangé une pomme.
I ate an apple.
Il est entré, a vu Paul, et a parlé.
He entered, saw Paul, and spoke.
Le téléphone a sonné.
The phone rang.
J’ai vécu à Paris pendant 10 ans.
I lived in Paris for 10 years.
Elle a fermé la porte.
She closed the door.
Nous avons fini le projet.
We finished the project.
Le train est parti à 8h.
The train left at 8 AM.
J'ai perdu mes clés.
I lost my keys.
Tu as compris la leçon ?
Did you understand the lesson?
Ils ont réussi l'examen.
They passed the exam.

Imparfait: Scenery, Habits, States & Continuous Action

The Imparfait handles continuous background actions, habits, states of mind, and scene painting.

Il faisait très froid.
It was very cold.
Je lisais un livre.
I was reading a book.
Nous jouions au tennis.
We used to play tennis.
Je pensais qu’il était tard.
I thought that it was late.
Les gens marchaient dans la rue.
People were walking in the street.
Elle voulait partir.
She wanted to leave.
Quand j'étais jeune...
When I was young...
Il pleuvait sans cesse.
It was raining constantly.
Je savais la réponse.
I knew the answer.
Ils buvaient du café tous les matins.
They would drink coffee every morning.

Passé Simple: The Literary Narrative Past

Functions identically to the Passé Composé (completed event), but restricted almost purely to literature, novels, and historical texts.

Il entra dans la pièce.
He entered the room.
Elle parla longtemps.
She spoke for a long time.
Le roi abdiqua.
The king abdicated.
Ils vécurent heureux.
They lived happily.
Je pris mon sac.
I took my bag.
La nuit tomba soudainement.
Night fell suddenly.
Il reçut une lettre.
He received a letter.
Nous bûmes un verre.
We drank a glass.
Le rideau tomba.
The curtain fell.
Elle gravit la montagne.
She climbed the mountain.

⚙️ The Master Flow Decision System (Daby Method)

Step Question Tense Choice
Step 1 Does the action move the main storyline forward? Passé Composé
Step 2 Is the verb describing a background scene, state, or mental condition? Imparfait
Step 3 Is it an action in progress (past continuous)? Imparfait
Step 4 Is it an action that was a past habit or routine? Imparfait
Step 5 Does an event suddenly interrupt an ongoing background scene? Imparfait (scene) + Passé Composé (interruption)

🧠 The Daby Camera Test

Ask yourself: "Can I freeze this moment as a complete, single photograph?"

Sentence: "Je ____________ (regarder) la télévision quand le facteur a sonné."

Answer: regardais (Imparfait) + a sonné (Passé Composé)
Why? Watching TV is the ongoing background video (Camera freeze: NO). The doorbell ringing is the sudden interrupting photograph/event (Camera freeze: YES).
